😱 Scary for a second, right?

Every "leaked" detail you saw was completely fake — just redacted placeholders designed to look alarming. But that panic you felt? That's exactly what real attackers exploit.

🎯 Lesson 1 — Never scan random QR codes

QR codes on posters, stickers, or emails from strangers can lead to phishing sites and malware. This attack is called "quishing" and it's growing fast.

🔍 Lesson 2 — Always preview the URL

Most phone scanners show the link before opening. Read it carefully. Watch for typos like g00gle.com or weird long subdomains.

🔐 Lesson 3 — Turn on 2FA everywhere

Even if a password leaks, two-factor authentication (preferably an authenticator app, not SMS) stops most account takeovers.

📵 Lesson 4 — Watch for sticker attacks

Attackers physically paste fake QR stickers over real ones on menus, posters, and signage. When unsure, type the URL manually.

🛡️ Lesson 5 — If it feels urgent, slow down

Panic is a hacker's best tool. Flashing warnings and countdowns aren't how real security works. Breathe, then verify through official channels.

Stay curious. Stay skeptical. Stay safe. 🔒
